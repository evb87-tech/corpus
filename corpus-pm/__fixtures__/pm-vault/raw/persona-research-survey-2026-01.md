# Persona Research Survey — Notification Overload in B2B Tech Teams
## NotifAI Internal Research | January 2026

**Respondents:** 87 professionals (engineering managers, devops leads, senior engineers, PMs)
**Method:** Online survey + 12 follow-up calls
**Recruiting:** LinkedIn outreach, referral network, PM communities

---

## Executive Summary

Notification overload is nearly universal among respondents (94% report it as a "significant" or "major" friction). However, the framing of the problem differs sharply by role:

- **Engineering managers (n=34):** Focus on *team clarity* — notifications that confuse junior engineers or create escalation theater.
- **DevOps/SRE leads (n=28):** Focus on *signal-to-noise* — too many alerts, too few that matter.
- **Senior ICs (n=25):** Focus on *focus preservation* — interruptions during deep work.

---

## Key Findings

### 1. The urgency signal is broken for 78% of respondents

> "Everything is marked urgent. Nothing is actually urgent."
> — IC, fintech startup, 45 employees

> "We had to introduce a 'super-urgent' label because urgent stopped meaning anything."
> — EM, logistics SaaS, 80 employees

### 2. AI digest tools have low trust (41% tried, 67% of those stopped using)

Reasons for stopping (multiple choice):
- "Summarized noise and signal equally" — 58%
- "Missed something important" — 44%
- "Didn't integrate with my workflow" — 37%
- "Privacy concerns" — 22%

### 3. Routing is seen as the highest-value intervention

When asked "what would reduce your notification stress most?":
- **Smart routing** (right person, right time): 61%
- **Better digest/summary**: 24%
- **Mute/snooze management**: 11%
- **Analytics dashboard**: 4%

### 4. Auditability is a purchasing criterion for 83% of decision-makers

Among respondents with purchasing authority (n=31):
> "I won't use a tool I can't explain to my team. If it routes an alert to person X, I need to see why."
> — DevOps Lead, e-commerce platform

### 5. Privacy concerns are a hard blocker for 29%

> "Anything that involves on-call schedules, availability data, or work patterns touches HR territory. Our legal team would need to approve."
> — Engineering Manager, health-tech

## Segment Snapshots

### Segment A: "Firefighter" (DevOps/SRE, 15-100 person orgs)
- Primary pain: alert fatigue, noise suppression
- Tool budget: €200–800/month
- Decision maker: themselves or direct manager
- Key concern: rules, not ML

### Segment B: "Orchestrator" (Engineering Manager, 100-500 person orgs)
- Primary pain: team notification clarity, onboarding overhead
- Tool budget: €500–2,000/month
- Decision maker: themselves + IT/security sign-off
- Key concern: auditability, privacy compliance

### Segment C: "Builder" (Senior IC/Tech Lead, any size)
- Primary pain: deep work interrupted by non-urgent pings
- Tool budget: personal or team budget €50–200/month
- Decision maker: themselves
- Key concern: integration with existing tools (Slack, Linear, GitHub)
